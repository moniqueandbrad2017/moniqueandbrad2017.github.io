The Bride and Groom

LADY G

Monique has been fortunate enough to have an extremely #blessed life with loving parents Reuel and Jennifer and siblings Jordan and Grace. Monique is the middle child and boy does it show! She grew up with her fam in SoCal, went to boarding scheeoul at Thacher where she learned tons-- including how to ride ponies and camping in the wilderness. She attended college in Cali and grad school on the East Coast, traveled the world, and now is residing in the rain of Portland. B

Brad grew up (mostly) in Park City, Utah with his parents Mike and Kathleen. From an early age, Brad has been passionate about challenges such as, but not limited to, endurance sports (Biathlon champ!) coin collecting, and mathematics (major and master degree)! Brad considers himself a middle child--but in reality he's an identical twin (Blaine) with a younger brother (Reid). Brad is also enthusiastic about playing and listening to jazz music. HOW WE MET

Way back in 2012, in the foggy city of San Francisco, there lived a Monique and a Brad who were gainfully employed at the same employer. On the same team no less! Monique and Brad worked very dutifully together and enjoyed attending yoga class in each other's company and running statistical experiments on video games. Once during yoga, Monique and Brad accidentally touched feet during three-legged dog and the rest is history. Also, Monqiue had one too many at a holiday party and - the poor guy - Brad had no chance. The rest was REALLY history.

THE ENGAGEMENT

Monique never thought that marriage was for her. Brad spent a lot of time convincing her and eventually she opened up to the idea on the condition that she get the finest Brad-made engagement bracelet in all the land. Not one to shy away from a challenge, Brad accepted. The proposal went down on bikes in the rain on the scenic Broadway Bridge crossing the mighty Willamette river in Portland. Like true nerds, there was also a videogame involved.

Staying in PDX

Airbnbs

There are boatloads of Airbnbs in the area. Some suggested areas are Kerns which is right by the venue and the Mississippi District . Those links will get you directly to Airbnb searches. If you are new to Airbnb, sign up through this link to get $30 off your first booking.

There are also hotels aplenty if you want to stab a knife right though Brad's warm beating heart.

Getting Around

Portland is alternative transportation friendly. Our public transit is called Max and you can pick it up right at the airport and use it to get around town.

There is a new bikeshare service called Biketown that provides bikes to ride around. It is super easy and fun.

There is also Lyft/Uber, which if you haven't used here is a link to get $50 in free rides.
